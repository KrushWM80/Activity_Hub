Restitution Checks Mailing Update

All restitution checks must now be mailed to one standardized address. Preprinted envelopes are no longer available.

Why It Matters

Sending restitution checks to one standardized address helps payments get processed accurately and without delays.

This gives courts, attorneys and external partners a clear and consistent location to mail all restitution issued on Walmart’s behalf.

Preparing Mail

Mail all restitution checks to:

Walmart Restitution Recovery

P.O. Box 7412016

Chicago, IL 606742016

Teams should send all restitution checks in standard envelopes.

Preprinted envelopes can no longer be used or requested.

Explore More

Go to OneWalmart > BetterWay > Supercenters/Division 1 > Asset Protection >  Investigations.

Summarized: Preprinted restitution check envelopes are no longer available. Associates must send all restitution checks to one address using standard envelopes.

DSL Supply Update for Stores Converted Before Feb. 2026

Stores converted before Feb. 2026 may experience delays in receiving certain digital shelf label (DSL) supplies due to low inventory and backorders affecting several adapters. Stores should continue placing orders through QuickBase, avoid duplicate requests, and review for misuse before ordering standalone tags, as those with excessive misuse will not be approved. Use the DSL Order Dashboard to track approved orders and stay updated on order statuses.

With hot weather conditions increasing the risk of heat-related illness, it's crucial for your teams to prepare by following safety tips like drinking water, resting in the shade and ventilating enclosed spaces. How to Improve Scrubber Performance and Run Time

Keep scrubbers running efficiently with simple daily habits. The Maintenance team should clean and check the squeegee, hose, sensors and tank after each use. As a best practice, members of management will know this is done right if they see the lids are left open for proper air drying to prevent odors and maintain performance.

Slip, Trip and Fall Safety

Spring break means more customers – and more slip, trip and fall risk. Plan cleaning coverage for busy times, keep orange Towel-in-Pocket supplies ready, review weather daily, and do quick safety sweeps to fix hazards fast.
