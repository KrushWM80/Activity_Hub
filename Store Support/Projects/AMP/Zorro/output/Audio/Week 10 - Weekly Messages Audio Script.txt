Fresh Area:

Keep the spotlight on standards in Department 80 to drive strong Service Deli sales. Focus on the basics and maintain high standards across the department by ensuring the area is well lit, fixtures are clean, set correctly and in good working condition, items are rotated, stocked, priced and presentable, inventory is organized and on hands are accurate, and all food safety guidelines are being followed in accordance with the Better Way guide. Finally, cut markdowns on pre-sliced deli meat and cheese by labeling and stocking only what is needed to meet the customer demand and don't forget to utilize the sampling program to entice customers and drive sales.

Easter is here! Let’s finish the season strong in Department 93 and deliver top quality Fresh Meat to our valued customers. Focus on Easter favorites: turkeys, hams, Cornish hens, boneless rib roasts and whole tenderloins and stock bunkers strategically throughout the day to keep them full and drive sales. Hold steady on Easter hams: The Saturday before Easter is one of the biggest days of the season with the average store turning the Marketside ham bunker about five times. Avoid early markdowns on fresh turkeys: Merchants will monitor sell-through and support with post-Easter pricing, lowering the price for whole fresh turkeys to fifty cents per pound after the holiday. Finally, protect quality with meticulous temperature management by maintaining the 30 degree target temperature. At 34 degrees, shelf life starts to diminish quickly, losing one day of life for each degree the temp raises. If browning appears or leaks are visible, check the temperatures immediately. Review the time-lapse temperature video for examples.

Harsh weather, extreme temperatures, pests and disease have impacted domestic crops in the South, Southeast and Southwest and Mexico. As a result, expect delays, low level inventory shipments and possible outs on the following items: berries (blueberries and raspberries), bok choy, Napa cabbage, carrots, cilantro, corn, cucumbers, green beans, organic lettuce, peppers (green bell peppers, jalapeño peppers, tomatillo peppers), Roma tomatoes, watermelons and other mixed vegetables. Most of these items will be spotty through week 11 (Apr. 11-17) and are projected to improve over the next few weeks. Continue to monitor inventory, fill gaps with available high-velocity items and maintain flexibility until the supply has returned to normal. Currently, corn supply is very low and will remain tight until mid-April. As a result, the timeline to transition bulk corn from the wet wall to modularized bins has shifted from week 8 to week 14. Check back here for weekly updates throughout the summer.
