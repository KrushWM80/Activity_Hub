## THE STRATEGIC SOLUTION

### Hybrid Platform-Product Operating Model

```
                    JUAN GALARRAGA
                    SVP, Store Operations
                    Acceleration & Support
                           |
        ___________________|___________________
        |                  |                  |
   FOUNDATION         BUSINESS           CAPABILITIES
   
   Data Platform      Store Analytics    Analytics Capabilities
   & Engineering      & Products         & Data Science
                      
                      Home Office        [Data Governance &
                      Analytics &         Quality - Optional]
                      Activity Hub
```

**Design Principles:**
- **Separation of Concerns**: Platform teams build infrastructure, product teams deliver business outcomes
- **Business Alignment**: VPs own specific customer segments (Stores vs Home Office)
- **Dual-Track Teams**: Separate "Run" and "Transform" squads within each area
- **Strategic Platform Investment**: Activity Hub as unified analytics experience

## ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

### VP #1: Data Platform & Engineering
**Mission:** Build reliable, scalable data infrastructure for all teams

**Core Responsibilities:**
- Data ingestion, integration, pipeline orchestration
- BigQuery data warehouse architecture & optimization
- Platform reliability, performance, SRE (99.9% uptime)
- DevOps, CI/CD, infrastructure-as-code
- API management & data services

**Team Size:** 55-70 FTEs  
**Key Deliverables:** Enterprise data platform, API layer, 24/7 reliability

---

### VP #2: Store Analytics & Products
**Mission:** Deliver analytics products and insights for field operations

**Core Responsibilities:**
- **Ops Metrics Portal (OMP)** — primary Store analytics platform
- **MyWalmart** mobile analytics for 1.6M field associates
- Store labor, inventory, and execution analytics
- Field performance metrics and operational dashboards
- Direct partnership with Store Operations stakeholders

**Team Size:** 70-90 FTEs  
**Key Deliverables:** OMP platform, mobile analytics, store insights, field enablement

**Why Separate:** Stores need mobile-first, real-time, operationally-focused analytics distinct from Home Office analytical depth

---

### VP #3: Home Office Analytics & Activity Hub
**Mission:** Deliver analytics products and unified experience for corporate functions

**Core Responsibilities:**
- **Activity Hub** platform strategy and development (strategic priority)
- Merchandising, planning, and supply chain analytics
- Finance and executive reporting
- Cross-functional insights and enterprise self-service
- Consolidation of fragmented analytics landscape

**Team Size:** 70-85 FTEs  
**Key Deliverables:** Activity Hub platform, merchandising insights, finance reporting, executive dashboards

**Why Strategic:** Activity Hub creates "apples-to-apples" comparisons across business contexts, enabling unified analytics experience

---

### VP #4: Analytics Capabilities & Data Science
**Mission:** Provide horizontal capabilities serving all business areas

**Core Responsibilities:**
- Advanced analytics, predictive modeling, optimization
- Analytics engineering (DBT, semantic layers, metric definitions)
- Centers of Excellence (Data Science, Visualization, Analytics)
- Innovation lab and emerging technologies
- Cross-functional enablement and training

**Team Size:** 50-65 FTEs  
**Key Deliverables:** Predictive models, semantic layers, standardized metrics, capability building

**Why Horizontal:** Prevents duplication, scales expertise, ensures consistency across business units

---

### Optional VP #5: Data Governance & Quality
**Mission:** Enterprise data standards, quality, and compliance

**Core Responsibilities:**
- Data governance, metadata management, data catalog
- Data quality monitoring and remediation
- Compliance, privacy, security (PII/PHI handling)
- Master data management and reference data
- Data literacy and governance training

**Team Size:** 30-40 FTEs  
**Key Deliverables:** Governance framework, data quality standards, compliance assurance

**Decision Point:** Start as Senior Director under Platform VP, promote to VP as organization matures

## KEY SUCCESS FACTORS

### 1. Platform Standardization
**From:** Fragmented landscape (Tableau, PowerBI, Excel, manual processes)  
**To:** Standardized platforms (OMP for Stores, Activity Hub for Home Office)  
**Benefit:** Consistent metrics, reduced maintenance, faster time-to-insight

### 2. Dual-Track Execution
**Run Teams:** Keep legacy systems operational, support existing users  
**Transform Teams:** Build new capabilities, migrate users to modern platforms  
**Benefit:** No disruption to business while accelerating transformation

### 3. Business-Aligned Product Teams
**Store Analytics VP:** Owns all Store/Field outcomes  
**Home Office Analytics VP:** Owns all corporate outcomes  
**Benefit:** Clear accountability, focused prioritization, stakeholder trust

### 4. Federated Operating Model
**Central Platform:** Builds shared infrastructure and standards  
**Distributed Products:** Delivers business-specific solutions  
**Benefit:** Scale efficiency with customer focus

## IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)
- Appoint VP leadership team
- Define team charters and success metrics
- Establish operating rhythms (planning, reviews, governance)
- Inventory existing capabilities and tools

### Phase 2: Organize (Months 4-6)
- Place existing teams into new structure
- Define reporting relationships with operational directors
- Establish prioritization framework (Store vs Home Office)
- Create embedded analytics partnership model

### Phase 3: Transform (Months 7-12)
- Launch dual-track teams (Run + Transform)
- Begin platform migration (Tableau/PowerBI → OMP/Activity Hub)
- Build semantic layers and standardized metrics
- Scale data science and advanced analytics

### Phase 4: Scale (Months 13-24)
- Achieve platform adoption milestones (80%+ on OMP/Activity Hub)
- Sunset legacy dashboards and manual processes
- Expand self-service capabilities
- Mature governance and data quality

## RISK MITIGATION

### High Risk: User Disruption During Migration
**Mitigation:** Dual-track teams maintain legacy while building new; phased migration with pilot groups

### Medium Risk: Prioritization Conflicts (Store vs Home Office)
**Mitigation:** Formal prioritization framework with VP-level ownership and quarterly planning cycles

### Medium Risk: Talent Retention During Reorganization
**Mitigation:** Clear communication, defined roles, career path transparency, retention bonuses

### Low Risk: Platform Adoption Resistance
**Mitigation:** Change management, training programs, embedded analytics support, executive sponsorship
