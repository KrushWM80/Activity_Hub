### ⚠️ GAPS & RISKS

#### Gap 1: **Operational Director Engagement Model Undefined**

**The Problem:**  
The analytics org chart shows VP → Senior Director → Director structure, but doesn't specify how **Operational Directors** (Juan's directors) interface with **Analytics Directors**.

**Example Scenario:**
- **Operational Director**: Director, Store Performance (reports to Juan's VP)
- **Analytics Support**: Director, Store Operations Analytics (reports to Analytics VP)
- **Question**: Who sets priorities? Who owns the roadmap? How do conflicts resolve?

**Recommendation:**
Create an **Embedded Analytics Model**:

```
Operational VP (Juan's Org)
├─ Senior Director, Store Performance
│   ├─ Director, Store Performance (Operational)
│   └─ Director, Store Performance Analytics (Dotted line to Analytics VP)
│       ↑ Embedded Analytics Lead
│       Reports to: Analytics VP (Solid line)
│       Embedded with: Operational Senior Director (Dotted line)
```

**Benefits:**
- Analytics lead sits physically/virtually with operational team
- Understands operational context deeply
- Translates business needs into analytics requirements
- Solid-line reporting to Analytics VP maintains technical standards
- Dotted-line to Operational leader ensures business alignment

---

#### Gap 2: **Prioritization Framework Lacks Operational Input**

  - Christopher Nuta or designated Senior Director (Operations Strategy)

- **Cadence**: Monthly
- **Purpose**: Activity Hub roadmap, cross-functional analytics priorities

#### Gap 4: **Triple Mandate Capacity Allocation by Team Type**

**The Problem:**  
The org design specifies RUN/TRANSFORM/INNOVATE tracks at **portfolio level** (30%/50%/20%), but doesn't specify how this varies by **team type**.

**Reality Check:**
- **OMP Team** (modernize platform): 20% RUN / 60% TRANSFORM / 20% INNOVATE
- **Embedded Store Analysts** (support operations): 60% RUN / 30% TRANSFORM / 10% INNOVATE
- **Data Engineering Platform**: 40% RUN / 50% TRANSFORM / 10% INNOVATE

Not all teams should have the same split!

**Recommendation:**
Define **capacity allocations by team archetype**:

| Team Type | RUN (Maintain) | TRANSFORM (Modernize) | INNOVATE (New) |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Platform Teams** (Data Eng, APIs) | 40% | 50% | 10% |
| **Product Teams** (OMP, Activity Hub) | 20% | 60% | 20% |
| **Embedded Analytics** (Operational support) | 60% | 30% | 10% |
| **Capability Teams** (Data Science, Analytics Eng) | 10% | 40% | 50% |

**Rationale:**
- Embedded analysts must maintain operational reporting (high RUN %)
- Product teams focused on platform migration (high TRANSFORM %)
- Capability teams should innovate (high INNOVATE %)

---

#### Gap 5: **Operational Metrics Ownership Ambiguous**

**The Problem:**  
Who **owns** the definition of "Store Performance" or "Labor Efficiency"?
- Is it the **Operational team** (Juan's org)?
- Is it the **Analytics team**?
- Is it a **Governance Council**?

**Risk:**  
Analytics builds metrics that don't match how operational teams make decisions.

**Recommendation:**
Establish **Metric Ownership Framework**:

```
Business Metric Owner (Operational Team)
    ↓ Defines business logic, decision thresholds
Analytics Metric Owner (Analytics Team)
    ↓ Implements data model, calculations, visualizations
Governance Council
    ↓ Approves metric definitions, resolves conflicts
```

**Example:**
- **Metric**: "Store Execution Score"
- **Business Owner**: Senior Director, Store Operations (Juan's org)
  - Defines: What "good execution" means operationally
- **Analytics Owner**: Director, Store Operations Analytics (Analytics org)
  - Implements: Data sources, calculation logic, dashboard design
- **Governance**: Data Governance Council approves semantic definition

  - Business outcome metrics (e.g., Store Performance improvement)
  - Analytics delivery metrics (e.g., Reports delivered on time)

### Enhancement 2: **Create "Analytics Business Partners" Role**

**New Role**: **Senior Analytics Business Partner**  
**Level**: Director-equivalent or Senior Manager  
**Reports to**: VP Store Analytics or VP Home Office Analytics  
**Embedded with**: Operational VPs (e.g., Christopher Nuta)

**Responsibilities:**
- Translate operational strategy into analytics roadmap
- Represent operational needs in analytics prioritization
- Escalate blockers between operational and analytics teams
- Facilitate Business Advisory Council meetings
- Measure analytics impact on business outcomes

**Why This Works:**
- Creates a "bridge" role between operational and analytics orgs
- Ensures operational voice is heard at VP level in analytics org
- Provides career path for analysts who understand both business and data

---

### Enhancement 3: **Clarify Activity Hub Governance for Ops Teams**

**Problem:**  
Activity Hub is owned by Home Office Analytics VP, but Juan's **Operations Strategy team** (Christopher Nuta) needs it for cross-functional coordination.

**Solution:**  
Create **Activity Hub Steering Committee**:

**Chair**: Senior Director, Activity Hub (Analytics org)  
**Co-Chair**: Senior Director, Operations Strategy (Juan's org)  
**Members**:
- Representatives from Merchandising, Finance, Supply Chain (Home Office stakeholders)
- Representative from Store Operations (Juan's org)
- Product Manager, Activity Hub
- Engineering Lead, Activity Hub

**Decision Rights:**
- **Operational teams** prioritize features and use cases
- **Analytics team** owns technical delivery and platform architecture
- **Steering Committee** resolves conflicts via voting or escalation to VPs

---

### Enhancement 4: **Define "Store vs Home Office" Prioritization Tiebreaker**

**Scenario:**  
VP Store Analytics and VP Home Office Analytics both want the same Data Engineering resource. Who wins?

**Current State**: Undefined (leads to political battles)

**Recommendation**:  
**Tiered Prioritization Framework**:

**Tier 1: Critical Business Impact** (Juan decides)
- Regulatory/compliance requirements
- Critical operational disruptions (e.g., Store dashboards down)
- Executive commitments (e.g., Board of Directors presentation)

**Tier 2: Strategic Initiatives** (VPs negotiate, Juan mediates)
- Activity Hub development
- OMP feature launches
- Data platform upgrades

**Tier 3: BAU Requests** (VPs manage capacity autonomously)
- Report updates
- Dashboard tweaks
- Ad-hoc analysis

**Escalation Path**:  
Product VP → Platform VP → Juan (SVP)
