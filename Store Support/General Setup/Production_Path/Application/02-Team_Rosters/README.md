# 🎫 Step 2: Team Rosters Product ID Creation

**Timeline:** 3-5 business days  
**Owner:** Data Quality Champion (DQC)  
**Prerequisites:** Step 1 completed (DQC identified)

---

## 🎯 Overview

Team Rosters is Walmart's central registry for product teams and engineering work tracking. A **Product ID** is required to associate your application with a recognized product team and work tracking system.

This Product ID will be referenced throughout the APM and SSP processes.

---

## ✅ What You Need to Do

### Your Responsibilities (as Application Owner):
1. [ ] Gather all required information (see below)
2. [ ] Provide information to your DQC
3. [ ] Answer any clarifying questions from DQC
4. [ ] Document the Product ID once received

### DQC Responsibilities:
1. [ ] Log into Team Rosters
2. [ ] Navigate: **Admin tab** → **Request Product Teams** → **Request Product Team**
3. [ ] Complete request form with provided information
4. [ ] Submit request
5. [ ] Notify you when Product ID is assigned

---

## 📋 Required Information

Gather this information **before** contacting your DQC:

### 1. Engineering Work Source
**What it is:** The system you use to track engineering work and tasks

**Options:**
- [ ] Enterprise JIRA
- [ ] Global JIRA
- [ ] ServiceNow
- [ ] Azure DevOps
- [ ] Other (specify)

**Example:** "Enterprise JIRA - Tech Portfolio"

---

### 2. Project/Component Name
**What it is:** The specific project or component identifier used in your work tracking system

**Guidelines:**
- Use the exact name as it appears in your work tracking system
- Include any prefixes or identifiers
- Be specific (not just "Backend" or "API")

**Examples:**
- "Store Support Dashboard"
- "AMP Data Integration"
- "Supply Chain Optimization Platform"

---

### 3. Product Team Name
**What it is:** The official name of your product team

**Guidelines:**
- Use official team name (as recognized by Walmart)
- Include full team designation if applicable
- Avoid abbreviations unless officially recognized

**Examples:**
- "Store Operations Technology Team"
- "Asset Protection Platform Team"
- "Customer Experience Engineering"

---

### 4. Pillar and Product Association
**What it is:** The business pillar and product family your team belongs to

**Pillars:**
- Tech
- Merchandising
- Supply Chain
- Finance
- People
- Realty
- Other

**Product Association:**
- Parent product or platform name
- Business domain
- Strategic initiative

**Example:** "Tech Pillar - Store Operations Product Family"

---

### 5. Additional Required Fields
**May include:**
- [ ] Team lead/manager name
- [ ] Technical lead name
- [ ] Organizational hierarchy (division → department → team)
- [ ] Budget code or cost center
- [ ] Start date of product team

**Note:** Specific fields may vary. Your DQC will guide you on additional requirements.

## 🔄 Submission Process

### Step-by-Step:

1. **You:** Gather all required information using template above
2. **You:** Send information to your DQC via email or Teams
3. **DQC:** Reviews information for completeness
4. **DQC:** Logs into Team Rosters portal
5. **DQC:** Navigates to Admin → Request Product Teams
6. **DQC:** Completes request form
7. **DQC:** Submits request
8. **System:** Routes request to Team Rosters administrators
9. **Administrators:** Review and approve request (3-5 business days)
10. **System:** Assigns Product ID
11. **DQC:** Receives notification with Product ID
12. **DQC:** Forwards Product ID to you
13. **You:** Document Product ID for Step 3 (APM)

---

## ⚠️ Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| DQC doesn't have Team Rosters access | Verify DQC is current and active in DQC Network |
| Work tracking system not listed | Contact Team Rosters support for guidance |
| Request rejected - incomplete info | Review rejection reason, gather additional details, resubmit |
| Product team already exists | DQC may need to request association rather than new team |
| Multiple components under same team | May need multiple Product IDs or single ID with component list |
