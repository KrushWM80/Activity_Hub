## Presentation Design Guidelines

### Color Scheme:
- **Primary:** Walmart Blue (#0071CE)
- **Secondary:** Walmart Spark (#FFC220)
- **Accent:** White, Gray tones
- **Success/Positive:** Green (#008000)
- **Alert/Attention:** Orange (#FF6600)

### Typography:
- **Headers:** Bogle (Walmart font) or Arial Bold, 32-44pt
- **Body:** Arial or Calibri, 18-24pt
- **Data/Numbers:** Arial Bold, highlight in blue

### Layout Principles:
- Clean, minimal text (max 6 bullets per slide)
- High contrast for readability
- Consistent spacing and alignment
- Professional charts and graphs
- White space for visual breathing room

### Animation Recommendations:
- **Main slides:** Fade in for bullet points
- **Clickable details:** Hyperlink to hidden slides, return link back
- **Charts:** Build data series one at a time
- **Transitions:** Simple fade or none (professional)

### Hyperlink Structure:
- Use "Details ↓" or "Click for details" buttons
- Hidden slides numbered (e.g., 6a, 6b for details under Slide 6)
- Include "← Back to main" link on all hidden slides
- Alternative: Use PowerPoint's zoom feature for detail sections

## Delivery Tips

### Opening (5 minutes):
- Strong hook: "$3.1M annual benefit, break-even in 2 months"
- Establish credibility and context
- Preview key decision points

### Body (15-20 minutes):
- Focus on business value over technical details
- Use hidden slides only if questions arise
- Emphasize ROI and strategic alignment
- Tell stories, not just data

### Close (5 minutes):
- Clear, direct ask
- Summarize key benefits
- Address obvious concerns proactively
- Open for questions

### Q&A Strategy:
- Anticipate questions on cost, timeline, risk
- Use hidden slides to dive into specifics
- Redirect technical questions to appendix
- Keep answers concise, offer to follow up
