## AT A GLANCE

### **The Challenge**

Execute three simultaneous mandates without disrupting business operations:

1. **MAINTAIN** — Keep legacy Tableau, PowerBI, manual processes running (30-40% capacity)
2. **MODERNIZE** — Build Activity Hub, OMP, MyWalmart as standardized platforms (40-50% capacity)
3. **INNOVATE** — Create advanced analytics, data science, new capabilities (10-20% capacity)

### **The Solution**

**Hybrid Platform-Product Organization**
- **Platform Teams** build shared infrastructure (data engineering, Activity Hub platform, APIs)
- **Product Teams** deliver business outcomes (Store analytics, Home Office insights)
- **Capability Teams** provide horizontal expertise (data science, analytics engineering)
- **Federated Governance** ensures standards without bureaucracy

## KEY DESIGN DECISIONS

### **1. Activity Hub Gets Strategic Focus**

**Decision:** Activity Hub has dedicated Senior Director + 30-35 FTE team under VP Home Office Analytics

**Rationale:**
- Strategic platform for Home Office consolidation
- Requires product ownership, not "side project" status
- Drives "apples-to-apples" metric consistency across business functions

### **2. Separate Stores and Home Office Product VPs**

**Decision:** VP Store Analytics owns OMP/MyWalmart; VP Home Office owns Activity Hub + functional analytics

**Rationale:**
- Stores need mobile-first, real-time, operational focus
- Home Office needs analytical depth and cross-functional insights
- Different user personas, different delivery cadences
- Clear accountability for business outcomes

### **3. Centralized Capabilities (Data Science, Analytics Engineering)**

**Decision:** VP Analytics Capabilities provides horizontal services to all product teams

**Rationale:**
- Avoid duplication of scarce expertise (data scientists, analytics engineers)
- Build deep specialization and best practices
- Economies of scale for training and tooling

### **4. Platform-Product Separation**

**Decision:** VP Data Platform builds infrastructure; Product VPs consume it

**Rationale:**
- At scale (300+ FTEs), "full stack" teams become inefficient
- Platform team focuses on reliability, performance, cost optimization
- Product teams focus on user experience and business value
- Clear SLAs and contracts between layers

### **5. Federated Governance**

**Decision:** Lightweight central governance (15-20 FTEs) with distributed execution

**Rationale:**
- Standards enforced through platforms (Activity Hub, DBT semantic layer), not policies
- Governance Council approves metric definitions but teams implement
- Avoid "ivory tower" governance that slows delivery

## CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS

### **✅ Must-Do Actions**

1. **Hire VPs First (Months 0-3)**
   - VP Data Platform and VP Home Office Analytics are critical path for Activity Hub
   - Don't start building until leadership in place

2. **Fund Activity Hub Properly (30-35 FTEs)**
   - This is the strategic bet for Home Office consolidation
   - Underfunding = "zombie project" that drags on for years

3. **Establish Governance Early (Month 1)**
   - Metric definitions, data quality standards, escalation paths
   - Prevents "rebuilding the same chaos" in new platforms

4. **Communicate the Vision Relentlessly**
   - The triple mandate is complex and counterintuitive
   - Over-communicate "why" and "how" to avoid confusion

5. **Track Migration Metrics Weekly**
   - Dashboards migrated from Tableau to Activity Hub
   - OMP adoption rates in Stores
   - Legacy tool decommission progress
   - Celebrate wins publicly

### **❌ Must-Avoid Pitfalls**

1. **Don't Build Full-Stack Silos**
   - At 300+ FTEs, every team doing their own data engineering = chaos
   - Platform provides shared services; products consume them

2. **Don't Starve the "RUN" Track**
   - If legacy breaks while building new, business loses confidence
   - Hard cap: 30-40% capacity on maintenance, ruthlessly prioritize

3. **Don't Let Activity Hub Become "Science Project"**
   - Must have business sponsors and real users from day one
   - Pilot with friendly stakeholders, not "big bang" launch

4. **Don't Ignore Change Management**
   - Thousands of users need training, communication, support
   - Budget 5-8 FTEs for change management or adoption will fail

5. **Don't Hire Legacy Skills**
   - Tableau experts → Activity Hub skills
   - Manual analysts → Python/SQL/DBT engineers
   - Hire for the future, upskill existing team where possible

## GOVERNANCE & DECISION-MAKING

### **Weekly Platform Council** (1 hour)
**Attendees:** All VPs + key Senior Directors  
**Purpose:** Operational coordination, dependency resolution  
**Decisions:** Platform priorities, incident escalation, resource contention

### **Monthly Architecture Review Board** (2 hours)
**Attendees:** Senior Directors of Engineering from each VP area  
**Purpose:** Technical standards, system design reviews  
**Decisions:** Approve RFCs, technology choices, architectural changes

### **Quarterly Business Review** (Half day)
**Attendees:** Juan + all VPs + business stakeholder Directors  
**Purpose:** Strategic alignment, priority setting  
**Decisions:** Roadmap for next quarter, resource allocation, major investments

### **Bi-Weekly Data Governance Council** (1 hour)
**Attendees:** VP Governance (chair) + Senior Director from each VP + business reps  
**Purpose:** Metric definitions, data quality, standards enforcement  
**Decisions:** Approve new metrics, resolve data ownership conflicts, set quality SLAs

## RISK REGISTER

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|:---:|:---:|---|
| Legacy maintenance overwhelms transformation | **High** | **High** | Hard capacity caps (40% max), automated testing, ruthless deprecation |
| Activity Hub adoption fails | **Medium** | **High** | Executive sponsorship, pilot with friendly users, invest in UX |
| Platform and product teams misaligned | **Medium** | **Medium** | Weekly Platform Council, shared OKRs, rotation programs |
| Talent retention during transition | **Medium** | **Medium** | Upskilling programs, clear career paths, bonus for milestones |
| Business stakeholders resist change | **Medium** | **High** | Change management team, "no worse than legacy" guarantee, exec comms |
| Store operations efficiency ROI doesn't materialize | **Low** | **High** | Conservative attribution (10%), track metrics quarterly, adjust course |
